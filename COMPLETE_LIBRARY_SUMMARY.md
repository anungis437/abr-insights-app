## üìñ Course Descriptions

### Phase 4 Courses (Advanced Topics)

#### Mental Health and Wellness
Examines the profound impact of anti-Black racism on mental health outcomes, diagnostic bias in mental health systems, barriers Black communities face accessing care, and how to build trauma-informed, culturally responsive mental health services. Essential for mental health practitioners, HR professionals, and anyone supporting Black wellness.

#### Decolonizing Practice
Explores the connections between settler colonialism and anti-Black racism in Canada, learns from Indigenous and Black solidarity movements, and develops decolonial approaches to anti-racism work that challenge Eurocentric frameworks and center marginalized knowledge systems.

#### Intersectionality Frameworks
Centers Black feminist thought and Kimberl√© Crenshaw's intersectionality theory to understand how race, gender, class, and disability create unique experiences of oppression. Addresses misogynoir, economic justice, disability justice, and practicing truly intersectional anti-racism.

#### Dismantling White Supremacy Culture
Uses Tema Okun's framework to identify white supremacy culture characteristics in organizations‚Äîperfectionism, urgency, individualism, defensiveness‚Äîexamines their harm to Black employees, and builds anti-racist organizational alternatives rooted in collective care.

#### Trauma-Informed Care
Applies trauma-informed principles to racial trauma specifically, recognizing racism as source of individual and collective trauma. Covers preventing re-traumatization in systems, supporting vicarious trauma for practitioners, and building healing-centered approaches.

#### Building Anti-Racist Organizations
Most comprehensive course on organizational transformation. Covers conducting anti-racism audits, developing strategic plans with measurable goals, building accountability structures, transforming HR practices, and sustaining commitment beyond performative statements.
